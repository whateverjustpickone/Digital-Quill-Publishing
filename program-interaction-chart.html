Lazarus Foundation of Mississippi
Program Interaction & Support Flow

PHASE 1: PRE-RELEASE ENGAGEMENT
Early Engagement Team
Works with individuals 12-18 months before release to assess needs and develop reentry plans.
Provides to other programs:
  • Candidate assessment data
  • Individualized reentry plans
  • Pre-release documentation
Pre-Release Services
Delivers reentry preparation classes, transitional planning, and benefits enrollment.
Provides to other programs:
  • Housing placement needs
  • Vocational interest data
  • Family reunification needs
Digital Literacy Training
Conducts basic technology and computer skills training in preparation for release.
Provides to other programs:
  • Technology skill assessments
  • Digital literacy certificates
  • Training recommendations
PHASE 2: IMMEDIATE POST-RELEASE
Lazarus House
Provides emergency housing and intensive case management for the first 0-6 months.
Receives from:
  • Pre-Release Teams (assessments)
  • Legal Services (documentation)
Provides to:
  • Lazarus Academy (eligible candidates)
  • On This Rock (job readiness)
Legal Services Clinic
Assists with documentation, record expungement, and legal needs.
Receives from:
  • Pre-Release (documentation needs)
  • Case Managers (referrals)
Provides to:
  • All programs (legal clearances)
  • Employment programs (background checks)
Initial Support Services
Connects participants to healthcare, mental health, and basic needs.
Receives from:
  • Pre-Release (needs assessment)
  • Case Managers (referrals)
Provides to:
  • Lazarus Academy (stable candidates)
  • Employment programs (job-ready individuals)
PHASE 3: STABILIZATION AND GROWTH
Lazarus Academy
Delivers comprehensive programming and vocational training (6-24 months).
Receives from:
  • Lazarus House (stable candidates)
  • Digital Literacy (training plans)
Provides to:
  • On This Rock (trained workers)
  • Lazarus Fabrication (trained workers)
  • Business Development (entrepreneurs)
Family Reunification Center
Facilitates family reintegration through therapy, education, and visitation.
Receives from:
  • Case Managers (family assessments)
  • Pre-Release (family connection data)
Provides to:
  • Lazarus Academy (family support)
  • Lazarus Village (family housing candidates)
Digital Inclusion Initiative
Provides technology access, devices, and advanced digital training.
Receives from:
  • Pre-Release (skill assessments)
  • Lazarus Academy (training needs)
Provides to:
  • All programs (technology support)
  • Business Development (tech skills)
PHASE 4: COMMUNITY INTEGRATION
On This Rock Construction
Employs graduates in construction, providing real-world work experience and income.
Receives from:
  • Lazarus Academy (trained workers)
  • Lazarus House (work-ready candidates)
Provides to:
  • Revenue (supports all programs)
  • Lazarus Village (construction services)
  • Community (improved facilities)
Lazarus Fabrication
Employs graduates in welding and fabrication, providing skilled trade employment.
Receives from:
  • Lazarus Academy (trained welders)
  • Business Development (contracts)
Provides to:
  • Revenue (supports all programs)
  • On This Rock (fabricated materials)
  • Community (quality products)
Lazarus Village
Offers long-term affordable housing options and supportive community environment.
Receives from:
  • Lazarus House/Academy (stable candidates)
  • On This Rock (construction/maintenance)
Provides to:
  • Garden by the Wayside (land/facilities)
  • Community (affordable housing)
  • All programs (success stories)
Business Development Center
Fosters entrepreneurship, incubates new businesses, and facilitates career advancement.
Receives from:
  • Lazarus Academy (entrepreneur candidates)
  • Digital Inclusion (tech-skilled individuals)
Provides to:
  • Community (new businesses)
  • Foundation (contracted services)
  • Revenue (business partnerships)
PHASE 5: LONG-TERM SUCCESS
Alumni Support Network
Connects graduates through ongoing peer support, mentorship, and community service.
Receives from:
  • All programs (successful graduates)
  • Community (volunteer opportunities)
Provides to:
  • Pre-Release (peer mentors)
  • All programs (success stories)
  • Foundation (advocates/volunteers)
Garden by the Wayside
Provides therapeutic agriculture, community gardens, and sustainable food production.
Receives from:
  • Lazarus Village (land/facilities)
  • All programs (participants)
Provides to:
  • Foundation (fresh produce)
  • Community (garden spaces)
  • Business Development (agricultural entrepreneurs)
New Beginning Ministries
Facilitates spiritual growth, faith connections, and church engagement.
Receives from:
  • All programs (participants)
  • Community (faith partners)
Provides to:
  • All programs (spiritual support)
  • Community (faith connections)
  • Foundation (volunteer network)
CROSS-CUTTING SUPPORT SYSTEMS
Revenue Generation & Financial Sustainability
  • Social Enterprises (On This Rock, Lazarus Fabrication) generate revenue to support all programs
  • Business Development Center creates entrepreneurial opportunities and community partnerships
  • Garden by the Wayside produces food for Foundation facilities, reducing costs
  • Development Team secures grants, donations, and fundraising to support all programs
Community Engagement & Partnerships
  • New Beginning Ministries builds faith community partnerships and volunteer networks
  • Alumni Network creates community advocates and provides volunteer support
  • Lazarus Village develops community integration opportunities
  • Business Development Center builds employer partnerships and economic opportunities
Data & Evaluation Systems
  • Pre-Release Assessment data informs program planning for all services
  • Case Management tracking provides continuous monitoring across all programs
  • Outcome Measurement across all programs informs continuous improvement
  • Employment and Housing Data demonstrates program effectiveness
Leadership Development Pipeline
  • Lazarus Academy identifies leadership potential among participants
  • Alumni Network develops peer mentors and program leaders
  • Enterprise Programs cultivate management and supervisory skills
  • Foundation Staff includes program graduates in key roles
Note: This chart illustrates how Lazarus Foundation programs interact and support each other throughout the reentry continuum. Each program both receives support from and provides support to other programs, creating a comprehensive ecosystem of services with multiple reinforcing pathways to successful reintegration.
